Overview

The appraiser plays a very crucial role in building the show. They are one part propmaster, one part casting director, and one part show runner.

From one hour prior to the start of the show, until 15 minutes before the show starts, they evaluate the items brought in by audience members. They are also sizing up the audience members themselves to determine where they will best fit into our improv/selling show, if at all.

Obviously this is all very subjective and is a major factor in determining how fun and successful a show is. Each check-in should only take 2–3 minutes max. Keep it moving while still making them feel valued. Stay playful but authoritative. Think of this process as part of the show.

Item Check-In

Access Requirements: The appraiser table or room can only be reached by paying admission first. In other words, a seller must be a paying member of the audience to sell an item.

Step 1: Form Setup

The appraiser has the seller scan the QR code and helps the seller/audience member fill out the form.

Step 2: Personal Information

The seller fills out the "You" section of the Merch Form. It's important to have an email address for payment purposes.

Step 3: Item Description

The seller fills out the item description.

Step 4: Sale Price

The seller fills out the Sale Price. The appraiser emphasizes that if their item does not sell, the seller goes home with the item and no money. The sale price is what the seller will get if their item sells. No more, no less.

They may want to set the price higher than they initially thought, but then it might not sell. The final price is their choice. It's a gamble for them. They are gamblers. We are the house.

Step 5: Pricing Information

The appraiser explains to the seller that the listing price is higher than the sale price because of site fees, credit card transaction fees and taxes. The buyer pays for shipping.

Step 6: Participation Discussion

Now the Appraiser and the Seller discuss the Seller's participation.

Step 7: Form Completion

The Seller signs and submits the form.

Step 8: Item Transfer

The appraiser takes the item, boxes it, and takes possession of it.

Policy: No item is rejected unless it breaks a rule of the selling service.

Note: If the person and/or the item doesn't fit (again, subjective) then the item goes into the lightning round. No consideration should be given to the salability of the item, only its comedic potential. Do not promise when their item will be used in the show. Do not promise who will sell their item, unless it's them.

Instructions to Seller: Simply tell them to listen for their item number to be called.

Process the Item

Step 1: Database Consultation

Consult the item database for information.

Step 2: ID Code & Performance Status

The item's four-digit ID code: Write it in large ink on the outside of the box.

Add a Y for YES will perform, N for NO will not perform, or M for Maybe will perform.

Step 3: Physical Organization

Stack the box so that the number and letter are visible.

Step 4: Script Documentation

Fill in the ID number and the letter in the printed script in the best scene you think it will work in.

Step 5: Enter Items into Whatnot

Enter the accepted items into the Whatnot system for tracking and sale.
