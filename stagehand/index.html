Overview

Our Stagehand is so much more than a stagehand. This is an on-stage performer with evolving responsibilities that blur the line between crew and cast. Throughout the show, the Stagehand:

  • Manages physical logistics during the show, including retrieving "sold" items, moving boxes, and prepping props
  • Delivers items to the stage before each scene begins
  • Works seamlessly to ensure smooth transitions between scenes
  • Acts as liaison between the Appraiser and cast/performers, signaling item readiness

Character Arc

The Stagehand's presence evolves throughout the show. In the first skit, they wander onstage like an actual FedEx employee who's stumbled into something unexpected, confused about what's happening. As the show progresses, they gradually become integrated into the performance itself, sometimes actively participating in skits, sometimes observing from the sidelines.

Between scenes, sound effects mark their comedic journey: the rumble of their delivery truck driving off with each "sold" item, followed by the screech of racing back, before they casually walk in carrying the next item. This running gag transforms a functional role into a beloved character who bridges the gap between backstage mechanics and onstage magic.

By show's end, the Stagehand has transformed from confused outsider to essential ensemble member, a meta-theatrical element that celebrates both the visible and invisible work of live performance.
