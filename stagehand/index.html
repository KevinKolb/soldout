The Stagehand Role

Our Stagehand transcends traditional backstage work to become an integral performer who bridges the gap between logistics and entertainment. This unique hybrid role combines practical stage management with comedic timing, creating one of the show's most beloved characters.

Core Mission: Seamlessly blend functional necessity with comedic opportunity, transforming mundane logistics into laugh-out-loud moments that enhance rather than interrupt the show's flow.

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Execute flawless item retrieval and delivery timing
  • Maintain the illusion of genuine FedEx confusion while staying performance-ready
  • Coordinate with the Appraiser on item readiness and stage transitions
  • Follow scripted delivery cues while maintaining improvisational flexibility
  • Manage physical comedy opportunities with props and set pieces
  • Serve as audience connection point between "real world" and show universe

Recurring Comedy Bits

BIT 1
Signature Collection
Always ask for signature confirmation. Attempt to read it aloud with increasing confusion - "Is that... Johnson? Jimbo?" Evolves from simple confusion to elaborate pronunciation attempts.
BIT 2
Truck Sound Effects
Coordinate exits with sound team. Escalating "peeling out" sounds from modest puttering to dramatic tire screeches. Match your walking pace to sound urgency.
BIT 3
Oblivious to Flirtation
Remain completely oblivious when recipients make passes. Respond with professional confusion - "Ma'am, I just need the signature." Earnest, helpful delivery person energy.

Bit Evolution Throughout Show: All three bits should escalate in complexity and audience involvement as the performance progresses. The signature bit can involve the Appraiser and audience in guessing games. Truck sounds become increasingly dramatic. The oblivious-to-flirtation bit allows the Appraiser to become bolder while you remain charmingly professional.

Show Timeline & Beats

Pre-Show (T-30 minutes)
Final costume check, prop positioning, coordinate with sound team on truck cues, review item order with Appraiser
Show Open (0-5 minutes)
First entrance with opening item. Establish confusion and "accidental" presence. Introduce signature collection bit with simple confusion. First truck "departure" should be basic puttering sound. Big comedic opportunity for audience first impression.
Mid-Show Transition (25-35 minutes)
Character awareness shift. Begin showing signs of understanding the performance while maintaining FedEx persona. Signature bit becomes more elaborate - maybe ask Appraiser for help reading it. Truck sounds start getting more dramatic.
Climax Integration (60-70 minutes)
Peak performance integration. Audience fully accepts you as show character, not just functional support. Signature reading attempts reach peak absurdity - might involve audience suggestions. Truck peels out with NASCAR-level intensity.
Show Close (80-90 minutes)
Final bow integration. You've completed the journey from confused delivery person to beloved ensemble member.

Technical Execution

Script Consultation Protocol

Essential: At each scene start, quickly reference your script copy to determine item reveal status - whether the item should be fully visible, partially concealed, or completely hidden during delivery. This consultation should appear as natural "delivery manifest checking" to maintain the FedEx character illusion.

Item Retrieval Protocol

When an item "sells," you have exactly 45-60 seconds to retrieve it from the stage area and position the next item for delivery. Coordinate with sound team - your truck "departure" sound covers the retrieval, your "arrival" sound announces the next delivery.

Delivery Timing & Script Consultation

Enter during Appraiser setup for each new item. Your arrival should feel natural and coincidental, not like you're waiting in the wings. Check your script copy at scene start to determine if the item should be revealed or kept hidden during delivery. The clipboard consultation and confused delivery process should take 15-20 seconds maximum.

Physical Comedy Opportunities

Approved physical gags include: clipboard confusion, package handling struggles, "checking your watch" during long appraisals, mock radio calls to dispatch, consulting your script copy for delivery instructions, and gradual realization of the show's nature. All gags must enhance, never distract. Script consultation should appear as "checking delivery manifest" to maintain character.

Recurring Comedy Bits

Signature Collection: Always ask for signature confirmation after delivery. Attempt to read the signature aloud, squinting at clipboard with increasing confusion - "Is that... Johnson? Jimbo? I can't quite..." This bit should evolve throughout the show, becoming more elaborate as your character grows more integrated.

Truck Sound Effects: Coordinate with sound team for your exits. Each departure should feature escalating "peeling out" sounds - starting with modest puttering, building to dramatic tire screeches by show's end. Your walking pace off-stage should match the urgency of the sound effects.

Oblivious to Flirtation: When delivery recipients (particularly the Appraiser) make romantic passes or flirtatious comments, remain completely oblivious. Respond with genuine professional confusion - "Ma'am, I just need the signature" or "Is there a problem with the delivery?" This bit works best with earnest, helpful delivery person energy.

Improvisation vs. Scripted Moments

Scripted Elements: Item reveal timing, specific delivery cues, coordination with sound effects, and key comedic beats are predetermined. Improvisation Zones: Physical comedy reactions, clipboard confusion duration, audience interaction moments, and recovery from technical issues. Always prioritize scripted timing while enhancing with improvisational flair.

Critical Timing Note: Your entrances and exits are precisely choreographed with the Appraiser's pacing. If running behind schedule, prioritize scripted delivery cues over improvisational comedic beats - the show's flow takes precedence over individual moments.

Audience Connection

You serve as the audience's "way in" to the show's universe. They see themselves in your confusion and delight in your gradual integration. Key interaction principles:

  • Eye Contact: Brief, confused glances at audience during early scenes establish connection
  • Shared Confusion: Your bewilderment mirrors their initial uncertainty about what they're watching
  • Growing Awareness: As you "get it," they feel increasingly in on the joke
  • Celebration: By show's end, they're cheering for your transformation as much as any other performer

The Meta-Theatrical Magic: You represent the intersection of "real life" and performance, making the entire show feel more authentic and spontaneous even as it's carefully orchestrated. The audience experiences the joy of watching someone discover they're part of something special.

Emergency & Contingency Protocols

Item Malfunction/Missing

If an item is damaged or missing: improvise with clipboard confusion, "check your delivery manifest," and buy time while backup solutions are implemented. Your confusion can cover almost any technical difficulty.

Timing Issues

If running long: compress delivery beats and focus on functional execution. If running short: expand physical comedy and clipboard consultation moments. Your role is highly flexible for show timing adjustment.

Appraiser Communication

Established hand signals: thumb up (ready for pickup), finger point (next item ready), closed fist (hold/wait), open palm (emergency stop). All signals disguised as natural FedEx delivery gestures.
